Key Principles for AI Success

1. Be Specific and Contextual

Vague prompts produce generic results. Include your role, audience, purpose, and constraints.

Weak Prompt

"Write a report about Q4 performance"

Strong Prompt

"Write a 2-page executive summary of Q4 2024 performance for TaylorMade leadership. Include revenue highlights, key product launches (Qi35 driver, TP5 balls), channel performance by region, and 3 strategic recommendations for Q1. Tone: confident, data-driven."

2. Iterate and Refine

AI conversations are collaborative. Start broad, then narrow down. Ask for revisions. The best results come from 3-5 back-and-forth exchanges, not a single perfect prompt.

3. Verify Critical Information

AI can make mistakes ("hallucinations"), especially with statistics, recent events, and company-specific data. Always verify numbers, dates, and facts before using in important communications.

4. Protect Confidential Information

Even with enterprise tools, exercise judgment with sensitive data. Avoid inputting customer PII, trade secrets, unreleased product details, or financial metrics without business need. When in doubt, consult Legal.

Workflows

Campaign Launch Workflow

Use this end-to-end process when launching a new product or campaign:

  1. Research Phase: Use web search to analyze competitor positioning, market trends, and audience sentiment. Ask Claude to compile findings into a brief.
  2. Strategy Development: Upload your research and product specs. Ask Claude to generate messaging frameworks, positioning options, and creative concepts.
  3. Content Creation: Generate all campaign assets: email sequences, social posts, ad copy, landing page copy, press materials. Use batch generation for efficiency.
  4. Localization: Adapt content for international markets. Generate translations and cultural adaptations for each region.
  5. Documentation: Create campaign briefs, stakeholder presentations, and tracking spreadsheets using Claude's file creation capabilities.
Content Calendar Workflow

Efficient process for planning and producing ongoing content:

  1. Monthly Planning: Share upcoming themes, launches, and events. Ask Claude to suggest content topics and a posting cadence for each channel.
  2. Batch Production: Generate a full week or month of content at once. Request all social posts for Instagram, Twitter, LinkedIn, and Facebook in a single session.
  3. Review & Refine: Review outputs, request revisions, and iterate on any pieces that need adjustment. Build a library of approved content.
  4. Export & Schedule: Ask Claude to format content for your scheduling tool or export as a spreadsheet you can import directly.
Competitive Intelligence Workflow

Stay ahead of competitor activities:

  1. Identify Competitors: Create a Project with competitor information. List primary and secondary competitors, their products, and market positioning.
  2. Regular Monitoring: Use web search weekly to find competitor news, product launches, and marketing activities. Ask Claude to summarize key developments.
  3. Deep Analysis: For major competitor moves, request detailed analysis: messaging breakdown, apparent strategy, potential response options.
  4. Report Generation: Generate monthly competitive reports as PowerPoint or Word documents for stakeholder distribution.

Prompting Frameworks

Choose the right approach for better AI results

Core Frameworks

CRAFT — Structured Communication

Best for: Professional documents, status reports, presentations

  • Context — Background and situation
  • Role — Perspective AI should take
  • Action — Specific task to perform
  • Format — Desired output structure
  • Target — Audience for the content

CLEAR — Similar Alternative

Best for: Performance reviews, executive summaries

  • Context — Set the scene with your role and situation
  • Length — Specify desired output length
  • Examples — Provide examples when helpful
  • Action — Be specific about what you want
  • Role — Tell AI what perspective to take

SIRE — Iterative Refinement

Best for: Complex documentation, technical specs, multi-step procedures

  • Specify — Start with your scenario
  • Iterate — Build through multiple rounds
  • Refine — Improve based on output
  • Examples — Add examples for clarity

Key insight: Don't expect perfection on first try. Plan for 3-5 iterations.

Socratic — Question-Driven Discovery

Best for: Exploring complex problems, learning new concepts, challenging assumptions

  • Start with open questions
  • Build on responses with follow-ups
  • Challenge assumptions
  • Explore different angles

Example: "Ask me 3 questions to help me understand the difference between OAuth and Basic Auth"

Choosing Your Framework

Learning new conceptSocraticQuestions reveal understanding gaps

Pro Tips

Start Broad, Then Narrow

Begin with a general request. Then refine with follow-ups: "Make it more concise" or "Focus more on the financial impact" or "Add more specific examples"

Provide Examples

Show the AI what good looks like. Paste in an example of the style, format, or approach you want. Say "Write something similar to this:" or "Match the tone of this example."

Break Complex Tasks Down

Instead of asking for a complete plan in one prompt, break it into steps: First analyze, then identify segments, then develop messaging, finally create channel strategy.

Ask for Multiple Options

Request variations: "Give me 5 different headline options" or "Provide 3 approaches to solving this problem, with pros and cons of each."

Use the AI as a Critic

Ask the AI to review its own work: "Now critique this and suggest improvements" or "What are the weaknesses in this approach?"

Always Verify Data

AI can "hallucinate"—generating plausible but incorrect information. Always verify statistics, dates, and facts, especially for external communications. Use web search for current information.

FAQ and Support

Common questions and how to get help

Frequently Asked Questions

Is my data safe when using these AI tools?

Yes, when using approved tools correctly. All approved tools are configured with enterprise-grade data protection. Always sign in with your @taylormadegolf.com account to ensure protection is active. Your data will not be used to train AI models.

Can I share confidential information with AI tools?

Use judgment appropriate to data sensitivity:

  • OK with enterprise tools: General business information, drafts, internal communications
  • Use caution: Customer information (only if business need exists)
  • Consult Legal first: Trade secrets, unreleased product details, contractual information
  • Never input: Passwords, personal financial data, employee SSNs

When in doubt, contact Legal.

How do I get a license for Claude/M365 Copilot/Gemini?

Submit a request through the IT Service Catalog:

  • M365 Copilot - Catalog #553 ($30/month, requires manager approval)
  • Claude - Catalog #575
  • Gemini - Catalog #588

Adobe Firefly is already included with your TaylorMade Adobe Account.

Can I use personal AI accounts (ChatGPT, personal Claude)?

No. Personal AI accounts lack enterprise data protection. Your conversations may be used to train models. This is a policy violation. Always use TaylorMade-approved tools with your work account.

Why are ChatGPT and Grammarly not approved?

These tools do not have enterprise data protection agreements with TaylorMade. Data entered could be used to train models or potentially appear in other users' outputs. We provide approved alternatives that offer the same capabilities with proper protection.

Why do AI tools sometimes give wrong information?

AI can "hallucinate"—generating plausible but incorrect information. This is more common with specific statistics, recent events, and company-specific data. Always verify important facts before using in communications.

What if I need to use a tool that's not approved?

Submit a request through the Generative AI Use Request Form (Catalog #590). Provide your business justification and use case. The Legal Department will complete an AI Checklist for review. Do not use the tool until it's been reviewed and added to the approved list.

What should I do if I accidentally entered confidential information into an AI tool?
Immediate Action Required

If you've accidentally entered sensitive, confidential, or proprietary information into any AI tool, take these steps immediately:

  1. Stop using the tool immediately - Do not continue the conversation or make additional queries
  2. Document what happened - Note what information was shared, which tool was used, and when it occurred
  3. Contact IT Security immediately - Report the incident to IT Security via:
    • Email: ali.chitsaz@taylormadegolf.com
    • Freshservice: Submit urgent security ticket
  4. Notify your manager - Inform your direct supervisor of the incident
  5. Contact Legal if needed - For sensitive matters involving customer data, contracts, or legal information, also notify:
    • Legal Department: ali.chitsaz@taylormadegolf.com
Prevention is Key

Remember: Always use enterprise versions of AI tools when logged into your TaylorMade account. Never use personal/free versions for work-related tasks. When in doubt about whether information is confidential, treat it as confidential and do not enter it into any AI tool without explicit approval.
